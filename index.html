Structure & Biography

Reflexive
Practice

A daily sociological inventory for awareness,
accountability, and growth.

"The only way of knowing a socially constructed world is knowing it from within. We can never stand outside it." — Dorothy Smith
Grounding Practice

Before beginning your daily inventory, ground yourself with this breathing practice. Find a comfortable position. Close your eyes or soften your gaze.

The practice will guide you through 5 cycles of intentional breath. You may stop at any time.

Prepare
Cycle 1 of 5
Section I
Personal Reflection

Inventory

An honest look at your actions, choices, and patterns today.

Did I act in ways that reinforced stereotypes, exclusion, or inequality today?

Did I use my privilege or position to challenge harm?

What truth did I turn away from today?

What harm did I look away from today?

Did I practice compassion toward myself and others?

Was I honest about my motives?

What did I do today that I wish I had done differently?

"The way to right wrongs is to turn the light of truth upon them." — Ida B. Wells
Section II
Relational Reflection

Dialogue

Examining how you engaged with others — in listening, challenge, and support.

Did I listen when someone's truth differed from mine?

Was I willing to be corrected or challenged?

Did I share my experiences, strength, or learning in good faith?

Who helped me see more clearly today — and how did I honor that gift?

Did I offer support or empathy without expecting anything in return?

"How do we hold people accountable for wrongdoing and yet at the same time remain in touch with their humanity enough to believe in their capacity to be transformed?" — bell hooks
Section III
Commitment & Closing Reflection

Integration

Translating awareness into accountability, and accountability into action.

What truth did I learn about myself today?

What amends do I need to make or acknowledge?

What small act of repair or solidarity can I practice tomorrow?

How did I practice awareness, accountability, or grace today?

Practice Complete

Practice
Complete

Show me what I reproduced and what I resisted today.
Help me move from awareness to action, and from guilt to grace.

This practice works best when done daily, but there is no failure in inconsistency — only another opportunity to begin again.
